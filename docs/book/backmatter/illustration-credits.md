# Illustration Credits {.unnumbered}

All artworks reproduced in this book are in the public domain and are drawn from
the collection of **The Metropolitan Museum of Art, New York** (metmuseum.org).

- **Cover** — *Celestial Globe with Clockwork*. Gerhard Emmoser (German), 1579.
- **Chapter 6, Containers & Supporters** — Ewer. Possibly by Johann Erhard
  Heiglen (German), ca. 1730.
- **Chapter 8, Light & Dark** — *Rothschild Lamp*. Andrea Briosco, called Riccio
  (Italian), ca. 1510–20.
- **Chapter 12, Readable Objects & Switchable Devices** — *Book of the Dead for
  the Chantress of Amun, Nauny*. Third Intermediate Period, ca. 1050 B.C.
- **Chapter 16, Custom Traits & Behaviors** — Desk and Bookcase. American,
  ca. 1811.
- **Chapter 17, Extending the Grammar** — *The Cloisters Playing Cards*. South
  Netherlandish, ca. 1475–80.
- **Chapter 20, Non-Player Characters** — *Finial in the Form of a Parrot*.
  17th–18th century.
- **Chapter 22, Turns, Timed Events & Daemons** — *Shiva as Lord of Dance
  (Nataraja)*. Indian (Tamil Nadu), ca. late 11th century.
- **Chapter 23, Scoring & Endgame** — *Ewer in the Form of a Phoenix*. Vietnam,
  ca. 15th–16th century.
