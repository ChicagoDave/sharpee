### ADR-172: Spatial Sound Propagation Model (new, ~480 lines)

Drafted from scratch, reviewed twice in single-ADR mode and twice in multi-ADR cross-seam mode. Final state: PROPOSED, READY FOR IMPLEMENTATION.

Key specification elements:
- **Discrete volume tiers**: whisper / subdued / normal / raised / shouting with integer budgets 1 / 2 / 5 / 7 / 9
- **Discrete audibility tiers**: silent / presence-only / fragments / muffled / full — coarse by design to avoid tuning hell
- **`AcousticTrait`** on the wall entity (whole-wall, per ADR-173 taxonomy): carries `baseCost`, `isOpaque`, `isOpen`
- **`AcousticDampenerTrait`** on obstructors (doors, tapestries, furniture): carries `acousticCostModifier` (positive = dampening, negative = more permeable — e.g., a peephole drilled through a wall, a hole)
- **Per-side acoustic modifier formula**: `effective_cost = baseCost + Σ(AcousticDampenerTrait.acousticCostModifier for all obstructors on the traversed side)`
- **`AudibilityEvent`** interface: `sourceRoomId`, `targetRoomId`, `wallId`, `sourceEntityId`, `content`, `volumeTier`, `audibilityTier`, `timestamp`; serves as the L2+ contract
- **Propagation algorithm**: single-hop only for Phase 1 (adjacent rooms); multi-hop via Acoustic Conduits (future ADR)
- **Acceptance Criteria**: AC-1 through AC-8, with AC-7/AC-8 paired to ADR-173 AC-9/AC-10
- **End-to-end scenario**: Study-to-hallway shouting + tapestry-puzzle dampening variant
- **Rejection Rules**: no continuous audio streaming, no 3D positioning, no auto-inference of walls, no direct room-to-room cost without wall entity
- **Future ADRs forecasted**: Acoustic Conduits, Active Acoustic Devices (composition pattern only), Visual Cross-Room Observation, Hidden State & Discovery

### ADR-173: Wall Adjacency Primitive (new, ~480 lines)

Drafted after wall adjacency was promoted from a structural gap in ADR-172 to its own primitive (David noted breaching, examining, decorating, and hidden passages all justify a dedicated entity model).

Key specification elements:
- **Walls as IFEntity**: authored explicitly, never auto-created; rooms with no exit and no declared wall are acoustically and physically unrelated
- **Room-pair cardinality**: exactly two distinct rooms per wall; one wall entity, both rooms reference it via `WallReferenceTrait`; bidirectional lookup supported
- **Whole-wall vs per-side trait taxonomy**:
  - *Whole-wall (intrinsic)* traits: `AcousticTrait`, `StructuralTrait`, `FireSpreadTrait` — properties of the wall as a physical object
  - *Per-side (interface)* traits: `WallDescriptionTrait`, `WallAdjectiveTrait`, `BreachBlockerTrait`, `ObstructionTrait` — properties of the wall as seen / interacted with from one room
- **Required per-side adjective with per-room uniqueness**: every wall side must have a distinct adjective within its room; forces disambiguation thinking at world-authoring time ("the north wall" vs "the oak-paneled wall")
- **`obstructedBy` state-coupling hook**: wall-side carries a list of entity IDs whose traits can modify wall-mediated capabilities; the wall queries obstructor traits and aggregates per capability (open-ended for future capabilities)
- **Generalized obstructor protocol**: obstructors carry capability-specific traits; the wall is the aggregator. This is the load-bearing extensibility mechanism — future ADRs for visual, olfactory, thermal effects compose via this protocol without modifying ADR-173's core model
- **Implementation Modules**: `WallReferenceTrait`, `WallAdjectiveTrait`, `ObstructionTrait`, `BreachBlockerTrait`; query helpers `getWall(roomA, roomB)`, `getAdjacentRooms(wallId)`, `getObstructors(wallId, side)`, `findTraitOnObstructors(wallId, side, traitType)`
- **Acceptance Criteria**: AC-1 through AC-10, with AC-9/AC-10 explicitly paired to ADR-172 AC-7/AC-8 for cross-ADR seam validation
- **Rejection Rules**: no auto-creation, no triangular walls, no wallless sound propagation, obstructors never own acoustic cost directly
- **End-to-end Scenario**: tapestry-on-north-wall-of-study as a multi-capability example (description, obstruction, AcousticDampenerTrait)
- **Future ADRs forecasted**: Acoustic Conduits, Visual Cross-Room Observation, Hidden State & Discovery (secret passages)

## Key Decisions

### 1. Sound as a Platform Primitive (ADR-172, Option B)

Chose a dedicated spatial sound model over a conversation-only helper or a per-room audio registry. Rationale: the "world sounds like a place that exists without the player" pattern pays off across mystery, horror, stealth, action, and domestic drama genres — not just The Alderman. Option B allows any future action or event to emit a sound, not just NPC dialogue.

### 2. Walls as IFEntity (ADR-173, Option B)

Rejected edge-metadata on exits and auto-inference from room layout. Rationale: walls are examined, described, decorated, breached, hidden-passaged, and used as sound/visual conduits. Treating them as first-class entities lets all existing trait and behavior infrastructure apply. Author burden is intentional — it forces explicit world-modeling at authoring time.

### 3. 100% Author-Driven Wall Instantiation

No implicit walls. Rooms with no exit and no declared wall are acoustically and physically unrelated. This keeps the platform honest: adjacency is a deliberate authorial claim, not an inference.

### 4. Generalized Obstructor Protocol (ADR-173 core extensibility mechanism)

Obstructors carry capability-specific traits (`AcousticDampenerTrait`, future `BreachBlockerTrait`, future `VisualConduitTrait`). The wall is the aggregator: it queries `findTraitOnObstructors()` per capability and sums contributions. Future ADRs for visual, olfactory, and thermal cross-room effects will compose with this protocol without modifying ADR-173's model. This was the key design correction in the session — the earlier "obstructions never affect sound" rule was too rigid for puzzle-grade gameplay (tapestry dampening, peephole, vent).

### 5. Discrete Tiers Over Continuous Values

Both volume (5 tiers) and audibility (5 tiers) are intentionally coarse. Continuous values create tuning hell for authors and unpredictable results for players. The integer budget system (1/2/5/7/9) was chosen to give meaningful gaps between tiers while keeping author-facing configuration readable.

### 6. Single-Hop Propagation for Phase 1

ADR-172 Phase 1 covers only adjacent rooms (one wall hop). Multi-hop propagation (vents, ducts, building-spanning pipes) is deferred to a future Acoustic Conduits ADR. This keeps the initial implementation scope bounded.

### 7. Future ADR Roadmap Locked In Both ADRs' Deferred Sections

Both ADRs forecast the same four future ADRs: Acoustic Conduits, Active Acoustic Devices (composition pattern — not a new primitive), Visual Cross-Room Observation, Hidden State & Discovery. This cross-referencing prevents independent re-invention of these primitives in future sessions.
