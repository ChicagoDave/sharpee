The Map Room

The map room sits at the heart of the archive, a circular chamber whose walls are lined floor to ceiling with yellowed nautical charts and survey maps. A brass astrolabe the size of a dinner table dominates the center, its rings tarnished green with age but still turning smoothly.

The only light comes from a cracked skylight three stories above, throwing a single dusty beam across the astrolabe's face.

A heavy oak door to the north stands slightly ajar. The passage south leads back to the grand entrance.

On the reading desk you can see a leather journal and a brass letter opener.
