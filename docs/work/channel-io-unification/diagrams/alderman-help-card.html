Investigative verbs
Six verbs unique to the case. Each one updates the board the moment you commit it.
CHECK <person>  |  CHECK <fact>
Reflect on what you know. The PC reasons aloud — no commitment, no edges drawn. Useful before you assert.
check catherine's truthfulness
IDENTIFY GAP  in  <person>'s alibi
Pin a hole in someone's account. Requires supporting evidence in the pile. Adds a new assertion to the board.
identify gap in catherine's alibi
CONTRADICT <claim>  with  <evidence>
Two facts in opposition. Draws a contradiction edge between the cited evidence and the claimant on the board.
contradict viola's rehearsal claim with the program
SUSPECT <person>
Mark someone for further attention. Adds the brass-glow treatment to their card on the case board.
suspect catherine
CLEAR <person | item | place>
Eliminate from consideration. Strikes the card on the board. Reversible — clear again to undo.
clear the revolver
ACCUSE <person>  with  <weapon>  in  <place>
The final accusation. You get three. Each one is permanent. The board's state at the moment of ACCUSE is what's judged.
accuse jack with the knife in the kitchen
Mouse · synthesized commands
Working the board
Every UI gesture round-trips as a parser command. The notebook stays the source of truth.
Click a card → issues CLEAR <item>
Right-click a suspect → issues SUSPECT <person>
Click an evidence card → highlights it & any assertions citing it
Hover an assertion → traces edges back to the cited evidence
