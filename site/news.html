July 2026
Sharpee 3.0 — Introducing Chord, the Story Language

Sharpee 3.0.0 is on npm, and it brings Chord — a story language built for writers. You author your game as a .story file: declarative blocks that read like the prose they produce, compiled to the same traits, behaviors, and phrases as the platform API. The classic Cloak of Darkness is under 100 lines of Chord (versus 785 lines of TypeScript). Version 1 of the language is locked, so stories you write today keep working. Two new packages, @sharpee/chord (the compiler) and @sharpee/story-loader (the runtime interpreter), join the family, and the CLI gains sharpee compose to compile and check your story. Upgrade with npm i -g @sharpee/devkit and meet the language.

July 2026
Sharpee 2.2 and Book Revision 2

Sharpee 2.2.0 is on npm, adding room-description snippets: you mark a spot in your room prose with a {snippet:name} marker, and the platform splices in text you wrote as the world changes around it — no string surgery, no re-describing the whole room. The Sharpee Author and Developer Manual is updated to Revision 2 to teach them, and the revision passed the same acceptance gate as the first printing: an independent first-time reader followed it front to back against the published packages — 31 chapters, 174 assertions, zero blocked, zero deviations. Grab the updated EPUB / PDF or read it online, and upgrade with npm i -g @sharpee/devkit.

July 2026
The Sharpee Book — v2.0.0 Edition

The new edition of The Sharpee Author and Developer Manual is out: 31 chapters that build the Family Zoo from a single room to a finished, themed, tested browser game on Sharpee 2.1. Before release, an independent first-time reader followed the book literally, front to back, against the published npm packages — repeatedly, with fixes between runs — until a full pass came back with zero blocked chapters and zero deviations. Read it online or grab the EPUB / PDF. The v1.5 edition remains available for the previous line.

July 2026
Sharpee 2.1 Released

The 2.x line is now the current stable release, at 2.1.1 on npm. Sharpee 2.0 introduced the phrase algebra, a rebuilt text-rendering layer that composes grammatical, article-correct prose from your world's vocabulary; 2.1 refined it, and 2.1.1 ships the fixes surfaced while validating the new book. Version 1.5.0 was the final release of the previous line. Upgrade with npm i -g @sharpee/devkit.

Sharpee 1.0 Released

The first stable, production-ready release of Sharpee is here. The Sharpee CLI is now on npm — install it with npm i -g @sharpee/devkit, then start a new story with sharpee init. After a long beta, the trait system, parser, and authoring workflow are settled and ready to build on.
