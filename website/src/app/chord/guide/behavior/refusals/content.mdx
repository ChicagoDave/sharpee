`refuse` stops the action and speaks a phrase. The conditional form
`refuse when <condition>: <key>` refuses only when its condition holds,
as the mittens line above shows. The bare form refuses always, for
things that are simply never allowed:

```chord
  on taking it
    refuse all-thorns
  end on
```

The named key resolves entity-scoped first (ADR-231): a
`phrase all-thorns:` override in the refusing entity's own `create`
block ([§2.10](/chord/guide/world)) wins over a story-wide
`all-thorns`, and a bare key
renders on standard actions just as it does on story-defined ones. A
dotted key is legal here too ([§5.2](/chord/guide/vocabulary)).

Refusing on a negated condition (`refuse when not …`) is flagged
(`analysis.negated-requirement`): that is a requirement in disguise, and
`must` says it better.
