Geography in Chord is declared as plain facts: rooms with exit lines,
doors referenced from those exits, and regions that group rooms and give
them shared behavior.

## Rooms and exits

```chord
create the Drive
  a room
  north to the Hall

  A gravel drive between bare lime trees.
```

An exit line is `DIRECTION to NAME` — the ten directions are the eight
compass points plus `up` and `down`. Plain exits are one-directional
facts; declare each side in the room it belongs to.

## Doors

A door is its own entity, referenced from an exit line with `through`:

```chord
create the Hall
  a room
  down to the Cellar through the cellar door

create the cellar door
  a door, lockable with the tarnished key
  aka grey door

  A grey door, locked as long as anyone can remember.
```

The exit line declares the geometry once — direction, destination, and
door — and the reverse exit is inferred as the opposite direction. The
door block itself is pure entity declaration: kind, description,
synonyms, behavior. It never names its rooms; a door's location *is* its
room pair.

Defaults follow IF convention:

- `a door` starts **closed** (`starts open` overrides).
- `lockable` on a door starts **locked**; name the key right on the
  adjective (`lockable with the tarnished key`). Start it the other way
  with `a door, lockable, starts unlocked`.
- A permanently open passage needs no door entity at all — just the
  exit line.

With that one declaration, OPEN, CLOSE, LOCK, and UNLOCK all work, and
the door is visible from both sides.

## Blocked exits are live conditions

```chord
create the Hall
  a room
  west is blocked while the boiler is off: frost-sealed
```

The `while` condition is read live — the moment the boiler comes on,
the way opens, with no events or extra wiring. The word after the colon
names the refusal phrase the player hears. An unconditional block works
the same way: `south is blocked: long-road`.

## Darkness and light

```chord
create the Cellar
  a room
  dark

create the oil lamp
  aka lamp
  light-source, switchable
  in the Hall
```

A `dark` room refuses sight-dependent actions until a lit light source
is present. Darkness is a fact about the room, light a fact about the
lamp; there is nothing else to wire.

## Regions

A region groups rooms and owns behavior for all of them:

```chord
create the Grounds
  a region
  containing the Drive, the Hall

  on every turn while one chance in 6
    phrase night-wind
  end on

  after entering it
    phrase cold-returns
  end after

  after leaving it
    phrase out-of-the-wind
  end after
```

`containing` lists member rooms (repeat the line freely; membership is
additive, and a region may contain another region). The block shows the
three region patterns:

- **A region daemon** — `on every turn` fires only while the player is
  in a member room; the `while one chance in 6` gate makes it
  occasional rather than constant.
- **Crossing reactions** — `after entering it` and `after leaving it`
  fire when the player crosses the region boundary, in either
  direction. Nested regions fire once per boundary actually crossed.

## First-visit prose

```chord
create the Drive
  a room

  first time
    The cab is already grinding away down the lane.

  A gravel drive between bare lime trees.
```

The `first time` paragraph narrates once, on the player's first arrival;
the ordinary description takes over afterward. Rooms only — it is the
natural way to open a game.
